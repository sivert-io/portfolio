## What I do

I work as a frontend developer at Norsk Tipping.

I build product features that people actually use. It needs to work well, but I also care a lot about how it feels. I spend a lot of time on small details that most people won’t notice directly, but they feel it.

## How I work

I mostly build things for myself.

Example: we had LAN events at work and didn’t want to sit there managing tournaments all day, so I built a system that does it automatically. Then I open sourced it.

I don’t like gatekeeping stuff. If I’ve already solved a problem, others should be able to use it.

I also really like automation. If something can be automated, I’ll probably try to automate it.

## Philosophy

I don’t take commissions.

I don’t want this to feel like a job outside of my actual job. I build because I enjoy it.

If someone donates and expects something in return, I’ll usually tell them not to. I’d rather keep it fun than turn it into obligation.

Also — I work at my own pace. I don’t respond well to people pushing or rushing things.

## Where I’m from

I grew up around mountains and cabins.

My family has a cabin without power (just solar), and we also have a farm in Gudbrandsdalen that we now take care of. It’s not like I grew up farming, but I’ve had to learn it over time.

Cutting grass, maintaining land, that kind of stuff.

I think that’s also why I like doing things properly instead of rushing through them.

## Cars

This is probably my biggest hobby.

Working on my car is basically therapy. I like just putting on music and focusing on one thing.

The main thing for me is detailing. I’m very picky about it.

- proper wash setup  
- different tools for different parts  
- foam cannon  
- interior care  

I go a bit overkill on it, but I like the result. The car looks clean, smells good, and just feels right.

I also go on a lot of drives with friends. Road trips, car meets, random drives just for fun.
