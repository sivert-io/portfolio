## Work and how I think

I work as a frontend developer at Norsk Tipping.

I build product features that people actually use. It needs to work well, but I also care a lot
about how it feels. I spend a lot of time on small details that most people won’t notice
directly, but they feel it.

Most of my projects start with me having a problem and fixing it for myself. Then if it turns
out good, I usually publish it. I don’t like gatekeeping stuff. If I’ve already solved a
problem, others should be able to use it.

A good example is the tournament platform I built for LAN events. We didn’t want to sit there
managing the tournament the entire time, so I automated it. That’s pretty much how I think in
general. If something can be done for me, I’ll probably try to automate it.

I also don’t take commissions. I don’t want this to feel like a job outside of my actual job. I
build because I enjoy it. If someone donates and expects something in return, I’ll usually tell
them not to. I’d rather keep it fun than turn it into obligation.

And yeah, I work at my own pace. I don’t respond well to people rushing me or constantly poking
me to do something right now.

## Background and life outside coding

I grew up around mountains and cabins.

My family has a proper Norwegian cabin without power, just solar, and we also have a farm in
Gudbrandsdalen that we now take care of. I didn’t exactly grow up farming, but I’ve had to
learn it over time. Cutting grass, maintaining land, that kind of stuff.

I think that background shaped a lot of how I am. I like doing things properly instead of just
rushing through them.

Cars are probably my biggest hobby. Working on my car is basically therapy. I like putting on
music and focusing on one thing. The biggest thing for me is detailing. I’m very picky about it
and probably go a bit overkill, but I like the result. Proper wash setup, proper tools, proper
products, clean interior, everything done right. I also go on a lot of drives with friends.
Road trips, car meets, random drives just for fun.

I also document a lot of the car stuff here:
