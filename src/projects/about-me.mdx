## Work & Approach

I’m a frontend developer at Norsk Tipping, working on product features that people use daily.

Functionality matters, but experience matters just as much. I spend a lot of time refining details
that most users won’t consciously notice—because those details are what make something feel fast,
intuitive, and reliable.

Most of my projects start with a problem I run into myself. I fix it, and if the result is solid,
I’ll usually make it available to others. I don’t see much value in keeping solutions to myself
if they can help someone else.

A good example is the tournament platform I built for LAN events. Instead of managing everything
manually, I automated the entire flow. That mindset carries over into most of what I build—if
something repetitive can be automated, I’ll try to remove it.

I don’t take commissions. I want to keep building as something I enjoy, not something driven by
external expectations. That also means I work at my own pace and focus on quality over speed.

## Background

I grew up around mountains, cabins, and a slower pace of life.

My family has a traditional Norwegian cabin without power—just solar—and we also take care of a
farm in Gudbrandsdalen. I didn’t grow up farming directly, but I’ve learned over time through
hands-on work.

That background shaped how I approach things. I prefer doing things properly rather than rushing
through them, whether it’s physical work or software.
